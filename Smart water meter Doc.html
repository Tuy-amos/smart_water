Automated Leak Detection

This is a technology that uses a variety of sensors, software, and systems to continuously monitor for and identify leaks in pipelines without the need for manual inspection.Traditional water meters require manual reading, often on a monthly while Smart water meters, equipped with automated leak detection, continuously monitor water flow.

Mult_Communication

To effectively manage water usage in a multi-unit building, smart water meters rely on a layered communication architecture, which allows many devices to send data to a single central application like smart phone and computer. Instead of each meter having its own connection to the internet, they form a local network within the building. This method is more scalable, cost-effective, and efficient.

Real-Time Controll

Smart water meter refers to the advanced capability to remotely and instantly open or close a valve on the meter using a digital command. Unlike traditional meters that require a technician to physically visit a property and manually turn a valve, a smart meter with this feature allows for immediate, on-demand management of the water supply through a mobile app or a computer dashboard.

Optimal Water Quality

The smart water meter system goes beyond simply measuring consumption. It transforms into a real-time monitoring station, providing measures of water's cloudiness, and measures the acidity or alkalinity of the water for ensuring public health, and optimizing treatment and delivery systems.

Project Hardware components

This is all hardware components that we use ih this project for completed smart water meter.

Microcontroller (ESP32)

This board is the brain of this smart water meter. It handles sensor readings as input and output, processes logic, manages Wi-Fi communication and making it the central processing unit for all operations that is used in this project.

LCD Display

A simple Liquid Crystal Display (LCD) screen used to provide real-time local feedback to the user, displaying status messages, warning indicators, and key sensor values directly at the device location.

Turbidity Sensor

This sensor is Used for water quality monitoring. It measures the cloudiness (turbidity) of the water by detecting scattered light, allowing the system to identify the presence of suspended solids or contaminants.

Water Flow Sensor

Measures the volume and Flow rate of water moving through the pipe system. This data is essential for tracking consumption, ensuring correct flow rates, and confirming overall system operation and integrity.

Solenoid Water Valve

This is the actuator Device of the system. It can be opened or closed electronically by the ESP32 to automatically control or shut off the water supply based on pre-defined logic or user permission.

Battery cell

This cell is used to supply power (+12V) to Solenoid water valve and Microcontroller.

Component ESP32 GPIO Pin(s) Function / Notes
Water Flow Sensor GPIO 2 (Input) Connect to the signal (data) pin of the flow sensor. Reads pulses.
Turbidity Sensor GPIO 35 (Analog Input) Connect to the analog output of the turbidity sensor. (ESP32 ADC1 is good for external sensors).
Temperature Sensor GPIO 34 (Analog Input) Connect to the analog output (NTC temperature sensor).
Solenoid Valve GPIO 23 (Output) Controls a relay module, which in turn switches the solenoid valve ON/OFF. (Requires a transistor/relay driver).
LCD Display (20x4) I2C: SDA (GPIO 21), SCL (GPIO 22) Most common way to connect character LCDs with I2C backpack (requires only 2 pins + Power).
LEDs (Indicator) Connect on relay circuit (Output) connect an external LED(red) on one Normal open of relay and other Led(green) as parallel to the solenoid valve.
Battery (with Power Supply module) 3.3V, GND Connects to ESP32's 3.3V and GND. Assumes a voltage regulator/charger circuit is used.
